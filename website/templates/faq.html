Frequently Asked Questions

1. What types of accounts do you offer?

Most banks offer a variety of accounts to suit different needs, such as checking accounts for everyday transactions, savings accounts for building your nest egg, and money market accounts for earning a little interest on your savings.

2. What are my ATM options?

Many banks offer access to a network of ATMs where you can withdraw cash and make deposits. You may be charged fees for using ATMs outside your bank's network.

3. How do I use online banking?

Most banks offer online banking, which allows you to view your account balance, transfer funds, and pay bills electronically. You'll need to enroll for online banking and create a secure login.

4. What are debit and credit cards?

Debit cards deduct money directly from your checking account when you make a purchase. Credit cards allow you to borrow money from the bank to make purchases, and you'll need to repay the balance each month to avoid interest charges.

5. What are bank fees?

Banks may charge fees for various services, such as monthly account maintenance, ATM withdrawals, and overdrafts. It's important to understand your bank's fee schedule before opening an account.

6. How do I keep my account safe?

Here are some tips:
* Memorize your PIN and don't share it with anyone.
* Beware of phishing scams - don't click on suspicious links or attachments in emails or texts.
* Review your bank statements regularly and report any unauthorized transactions immediately.
